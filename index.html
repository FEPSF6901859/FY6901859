Abstract

Increasing efficiency in the chemical and pharmaceutical sectors is vital to reduce CO2 emissions, increase supply for consumers and to improve chemical processes. In the UK, offshore wind energy is the most efficient (and renewable) energy source due to the environmental conditions. By 2050, the UK aims to reduce 80% of total CO2 emissions so it is important to use renewable sources in these sectors. AI in management enhances operational efficiency and decision making in pharmaceuticals. An example is using GenAI in clinical decisions and drug discovery. The implication of AI can increase efficiency can be seen to reduce costs and increase supply by providing atomization. Implementing catalysts in chemical processes can reduce CO2 emissions by decreasing the amount of waste products from processes. These improvements in the two sectors would increase availability and reduce prices of important drugs, improve supply chain management and reduce CO2 emissions.

Introduction

In this study, industrial methods will be examined to increase efficiency in these two sectors to improve important issues phased in the UK.

Use of Renewable energy to increase efficiency in the Pharmacuetical and Chemical sectors

The Uk industrial and manufacturing sectors are facing targets to reduce CO2 emissions by 80% by 2050 (compared to 1990 levels). Since energy consumption is the main source of CO2 emissions, improving energy efficiency in energy intensive sectors is key to achieving the target for 2050. However, energy consumption is unlikely to meet the targets unless new technologies and techniques are developed by businesses. (https://www-sciencedirect-com.surrey.idm.oclc.org/science/article/pii/S1364032118304775#s0005) The chemical industry is one of the largest industrial energy consumers in the UK, accumulating to around 12-15% of the total industrial energy consumption, this includes heating, cooling and running energy intensive reaction. The pharmaceutical industry (being a much smaller sector) accumulates 2-4% of total industrial energy consumption in the UK. Using renewable energy sources in these sectors would decrease running costs, create a more sustainable business and reduce energy demand. In the UK, the most viable renewable energy is offshore turbine farms due to higher average wind speeds compared to inland speeds

Implementing new industrial techqniques to increase efficiency in the chemical sector

The use of catalysts can be used in chemical processes to increase efficiency. Catalysts reduce activation energy (energy needed to make the reaction) by providing an alternative pathway by being used up in the reaction but when the total reaction is complete the catalyst is unchanged being able to be recycled for further reactions (https://r3.vlereader.com/Reader?ean=9780470011911).The choice of catalyst is crucially important due to catalysts being unique to certain reactions. This can cause catalysts that are rare to be costly for a chemical company such as Palladium, Rhodium and Platinum. A new innovative chemical called polyamide 6 (PA6) can recover expensive catalysts such as Platinum and Palladium. (https://www-sciencedirect-com.surrey.idm.oclc.org/science/article/pii/S0301479724030330#fig2) Providing an alternative pathway catalyst can cause otherwise harmful waste products to less harmful products (seen in a catalytic converter). Investments to higher quality catalysts in the chemical industry can reduce the yield of waste plastics, biomass and sludge.
